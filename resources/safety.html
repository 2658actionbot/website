Safety First

Safety Overview

Working with tools and machinery can be very dangerous. Our team takes heavy precautions to promote safety and prevent accidents in our facilities at all times. For more information, contact the Safety Lead.

Wheels

There are many different options for wheels to use on a robot. Different wheels apply to different drivetrains or mechanisms. Some drivetrains like mecanum, kiwi, and octoconum only work with the wheels they are designed to work with. However, other drivetrains like four, six, and eight wheel drive require the team to choose what types of wheels are best for the game and situation. Wheels are also used on mechanisms such as shooters and intakes. In mechanisms, different wheels are used depending on the different game pieces and situations. This resource page is meant to show all the wheels that can be used and to allow a basis off of which teams can make wheel choices for their robot.

Goggles

Goggles are mandatory at all FIRST events as well as at team events. You are not permitted to enter any pit or field without goggles.

Hearing Protection

Hearing protection such as noise cancelling headphones must be used when hearing loud power tools for your protection. Don't take any risks.

Tools

Check tools prior to usage to ensure that they are working. Know what they are and how to use them. If a tool isn't working, discard it. Make sure tools are safely stowed away when not in use. If you have any questions, ask a lead.

Shoes

Closed toed shoes must be worn within the working environment at all times as well as at FIRST events. This includes all onsite and offsite activities.

Batteries

Omni wheels are wheels with rollers placed perpendicular to the axis of rotation of the wheel. These rollers allow the wheel and robot to glide with little to no traction. These are great for scenarios where you want the wheel(s) to slide around and give the robot more maneuverability. These are sometimes placed as the front two wheels in a standard robot drivetrain so that turning is easier. Omni wheels are almost never used in mechanisms.

Soldering

Use lead-free solders with electrically heated soldering irons only. Wear face protection at all times and keep fumes away by working in well-vented areas. Work on a fire resistant surface.

Chemicals

Keep chemicals in good condition with legible labels. Follow the safety precautions and storage instructions of the MSDS. Do not use highly flammable materials at any time.

Competition

No team structures above 10 feet can be made nor support people. Always work on stable surfaces and take care of materials. Make sure the robot is off before handling it and that someone is watching. Lift the robot with 2 people and always deenergize before working on it. Open the circuit breaker and unplug the batteries. Be sure to vent compressed air in pneumatic systems.
